Uses of VR

Work

Companies are using Virtual Reality for all types of job positions, commonly software development, or engineering.

Recreational

Those software development jobs are used to make fun interactive and immersive games that put you in the eyes of a playable character in a VR world.

Military

The military and government communities also use virtual and augmented reality to train officers to operate expensive equipment, like aircraft, without the risk of damage costs.

Other

You can use VR for all sorts of tasks. Ranging from some fun video games, to it being used in the medical field. You can use VR to excersize, and you can be social in VR.

About

Most Well known Game

Beat Saber is a music/rhythm based VR game, where you wield two star wars style light sabers, and slash at the multi colored directional blocks approaching you at full speed. It is the most succsesful VR game to be developed, and is a great introduction to Virtual Reality.

Media Influenced

Developed by Owlchemy labs, Rick and Morty: Virtual Rickality is a interactive experiance influenced by the popular adult TV show "Rick and Morty." The game has the same voice actors, and is just as humorous as the show.

Biggest VR company

Oculus Labs is the most popular, and succseful Virtual and Augmented Reality company, starting out with the original oculus rift, debuting in 2016, and newly developing the Rift 2 in 2020.

First form of VR

In 1968, an apperatus nicknamed "Sword of Damocles was produced by Ivan Sutherland. It was so heavy, it had to be suspended by the ceiling, giving its nickname, Sword of Damocles
